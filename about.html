SMALL BUSINESS SATURDAY®

HELPING SMALL BUSINESSES
DO MORE BUSINESS

In 2010, American Express founded Small Business Saturday to help businesses with their most pressing need — getting more customers. The day encourages people to shop at small businesses on the Saturday after Thanksgiving. Since its start, the single day has grown into a powerful movement, and more people are taking part than ever before. This year, the big day is Nov 29.

THE BIG DAY BEGAN

The first-ever Small Business Saturday was held on Nov 27. It encouraged people across the country to support small, local businesses, and started a holiday shopping tradition.

THE DAY BECAME OFFICIAL

From Washington, D.C., to Washington State, governors, mayors, senators, and even President Obama all voiced their support for Small Business Saturday.

SMALL BUSINESSES OWNED THE DAY

American Express encouraged small business owners to take charge of the day, helping them promote their businesses through free personalized ads, appearing millions of times across the web.

NEIGHBORHOODS CELEBRATED THE DAY

More neighborhoods than ever celebrated Small Business Saturday, with individuals and local organizations pledging to support the day as Neighborhoods Champions.
